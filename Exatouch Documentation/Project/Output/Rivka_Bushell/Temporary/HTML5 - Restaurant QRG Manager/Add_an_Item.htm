Add an Item

An item is an offering that a merchant provides to their customer. Your business's list of items makes up your Menu/Inventory. For example, your restaurant may offer a Garden Salad on the menu. Garden Salad would be an example of an item you would add to your Menu/Inventory.

As part of 's comprehensive service offering, we provide complimentary Menu/Inventory import service. When you first sign up for , our in-house specialists will program your Menu and import your Inventory. If you choose not to use our Menu/Inventory import service, your Items List will be blank and you'll need to start adding items to your system. Even after your Menu/Inventory import is complete, you'll probably want to add additional items to your system.

How to Add an Item

1. From the Home screen Items.

2. Select Item Details.

3. Fill in the item details.

Notable Fields:

These fields ask you to enter important information about your item.

SKU: SKU numbers are barcodes. Barcodes can be scanned into the system using your barcode scanner, or barcodes can be entered by manually typing the the code into the SKU field.

SKU numbers are usually assigned by the manufacturer, but you can or generate your own custom SKU number by clicking .

See How to Generate SKU Numbers locally

Alt SKU: Applying an Alt SKU in assigning an alternate barcode to an item. For example, you may want to apply an additional SKU to a holiday edition of an item.

Short Description: This is the name of the item as it appears on your register's tab and on your customer's receipt. Short description also appears on your Items List and in your reports. The contents of this field are visible to your client.

Description: You can enter a longer description of your item in this field. The contents of this field are for internal use only; they will not be seen by your clients.

Category and SubCategory fields help you organize and keep track of your items.

Categories and subcategories are created on the Categories screen:

Click Home> Items> Categories.

See How to Create Categories and Subcategories.

Note: If you do not select a Category, your item will be assigned the Category: None.

Retail Price: Enter your retail price here. If this field is left blank, your item will be assigned the retail price of $0.

Optional Fields:

These fields are optional, but using these fields will help you track your items and inventory. You'll also be able to set up restrictions and tax rules for your items.

Manufacturer: Click to add a new manufacturer, or you can add a new manufacturer by clicking:

Home > Settings> System > Custom Labels > Manufacturer

See How to Add a Manufacturer

Sell options allows you set a condition for the sale of your item.

For example, if the sell option is Stop selling at 0, will not allow the sale of the item when your inventory for that item is 0.

There are several Sell options available:

Not an inventory item is the default setting in . Not an inventory item is used if you are not tracking inventory for the item. For example, drop-ship items which are special ordered and not kept in stock are not an inventory item. Most restaurants do not consider their menu selections inventory items.

Stop selling at 0 will limit the sale of the item once inventory reaches 0.

Go negative will allow the sale of the item if the inventory is 0. This option is useful for those times when your stock exceeds your inventory total. You can always modify your inventory to reflect additional stock..

See Modify Inventory Level

Each item can be assigned a Sell Option. Sell options are modified in the Preferences section.

See Modify Sell Options

Tax: The application of tax is defined through selection of a Tax Group.

You can select multiple Tax Group check boxes (i.e. Group A and Group B are both checked), so that multiple tax rates apply to a single item.

Tax rates for each Group are defined in Settings:

Home > Settings > Taxes

See: How to Set Tax Rates

When you've completed entering the item details, tap/click Save.

Tip: If this is your first time adding an item, or you are training a new employee, you can practice adding items in Training Mode. Training Mode acts as a sandbox, allowing you 'play around' in your system and add sample items without affecting your real-time data. See: Using Training Mode
